Letter to our Stakeholders

Conservation in the 21st century is no longer just about boundaries and guards; it is about radical transparency and community integration. In 2024, Jhalana Wilds has reached a critical tipping point where the economic value of a living leopard far outweighs any other land use for the local people.

Our success this year in stabilizing the population to over 42 individuals—one of the highest densities in the world—is a testament to the "Wilderness Luxe" model. By providing a premium experience for travelers, we generate the capital necessary to deploy military-grade surveillance and fund sustainable healthcare for the 14 villages bordering the park.

As we look toward 2025, our focus shifts to genetic diversity and corridor security. We are not just protecting a park; we are safeguarding a legacy. Thank you for your continued trust and partnership.

Rajesh V. Singh
